# Sugcar settings and reachability refinement

## Goal

Make the settings slide feel like a deliberate paired-device composition, strengthen Sugcar’s identity on the cover, and show that voice access matters outside the car too.

## Slide 1: cover identity

Show the supplied Sugcar icon in the cover copy, adjacent to the personal-project label. It should establish the product identity without covering the phone screenshot or adding a second wordmark.

## Settings slide

Use a centred flex pair rather than two full-width grid columns. The frames share a compact gap and one vertical baseline.

The settings frame has its own radius scale: the inner screen radius is slightly smaller than the small device shell, unlike the hero frame. This keeps the screenshot corners naturally inside the physical device outline.

## Phone-away use case

Add a short standalone slide after the driving problem and before the data-source slide.

- Eyebrow: `Beyond the car`
- Title: `When the phone is out of reach.`
- Body: `Ask for a quick reading when the phone is across the room—especially at night, without reaching for or waking the device.`

The visual is a quiet, dark bedside-style voice card. It uses the same Siri phrasing as the deck and remains intentionally lightweight: the point is a second context, not another feature claim.

## Quality checks

- Settings frames have matching top and bottom coordinates.
- The inter-frame gap is compact and cannot expand with the slide width.
- Small settings-frame radii are scoped away from the hero frame.
- The cover includes the real brand icon.
- The deck becomes eight slides and navigation/progress reflect that count.
